WORKPLACE CONDUCT AND CODE OF ETHICS

PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT STANDARDS
All employees are expected to maintain professional conduct and treat colleagues with respect. This includes punctuality, reliability, and professionalism in all interactions with colleagues and customers.

CONFIDENTIALITY AGREEMENT
Employees must sign a confidentiality agreement protecting company intellectual property, customer data, and business strategies. Breaching confidentiality can result in immediate termination and legal action.

CONFLICT OF INTEREST POLICY
Employees must disclose any potential conflicts of interest. This includes financial interests in competing companies, relationships with competitors, or personal relationships that could affect objectivity.

USE OF COMPANY ASSETS
Company equipment and resources (computers, phones, software) are for business purposes only. Personal use should be minimal and not interfere with work. Company monitors usage for security compliance.

SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY
Employees should not post confidential company information on social media. Negative posts about the company, customers, or colleagues can result in disciplinary action. Personal social media conduct reflecting poorly on company may be addressed.

SUBSTANCE ABUSE POLICY
The company maintains a drug-free workplace. Employees are prohibited from being under the influence of illegal drugs or alcohol at work. Violation can result in immediate termination. Random drug testing may be conducted.

VIOLENCE AND WEAPONS POLICY
Violence, threats of violence, or weapons of any kind are absolutely prohibited at work. Any credible threat will result in immediate termination and referral to law enforcement.

ATTENDANCE AND PUNCTUALITY
Employees are expected to arrive on time and be present for their scheduled hours. Excessive tardiness or absenteeism can result in disciplinary action. Patterns are documented by HR.

DRESS CODE POLICY
The company maintains a business casual dress code. Jeans are not permitted except on designated "Casual Fridays". Employees in customer-facing roles must dress more formally.

PERFORMANCE EXPECTATIONS
All employees are expected to meet performance goals set in their job descriptions. Annual reviews assess performance against these goals. Underperformance may result in performance improvement plans (PIPs).

CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION HANDLING
Sensitive company information must be stored securely and not shared outside the company. When working remotely, all confidential files must be stored on company-encrypted devices.

EXIT PROCEDURES
Upon resignation or termination, employees must return all company property, provide knowledge transfer documentation, and sign an exit agreement. Final paycheck includes accrued PTO.

COMPENSATION, PERFORMANCE, AND CAREER DEVELOPMENT

PERFORMANCE REVIEW CYCLE
Annual performance reviews are conducted in March. Mid-year check-ins occur in September. Reviews include self-assessment, manager feedback, and goal setting for the next period.

PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLAN (PIP)
Employees with performance concerns may be placed on a PIP for 30-90 days. The PIP outlines specific improvement targets, support provided, and consequences of failure to improve.

PROMOTION CRITERIA
Promotions are based on performance, tenure, skills, and availability of positions. Internal candidates are considered first. Managers must submit promotion requests with justification to HR.

CAREER DEVELOPMENT
The company encourages career growth through training, mentorship, and stretch assignments. Employees should discuss career goals with their managers during annual reviews.

MERIT INCREASE GUIDELINES
Merit increases are awarded during annual reviews based on performance ratings. Average increase is 3-5%. High performers may receive higher increases, while underperformers may receive no increase.

BONUS STRUCTURE
The company awards discretionary bonuses based on company performance and individual contributions. Bonuses are typically awarded in December. Eligibility requires employment through bonus payout date.

COMMISSION STRUCTURE (Sales Roles)
Sales employees earn a base salary plus commission. Commission structure is based on revenue targets. Commission is calculated and paid monthly.

PERFORMANCE RATING SCALE
Employees are rated on a 5-point scale: Exceeds Expectations, Meets Expectations, Developing, Below Expectations, Unsatisfactory. Ratings drive promotion and compensation decisions.

RETENTION BONUSES
Employees with critical skills may receive retention bonuses tied to staying with the company for a specified period. Conditions are documented in writing and separately agreed upon.

ROLE-SPECIFIC COMPENSATION
Different roles have different compensation structures. Technical roles may have higher salaries. Sales roles include commissions. Leadership roles include performance bonuses.

SALARY BENCHMARKING
The company conducts annual salary benchmarking against industry standards. Adjustments are made to stay competitive in the talent market. Benchmarking data is confidential.

QUESTIONS ABOUT COMPENSATION
Employees with compensation questions should contact HR directly. Salary information is confidential and should not be discussed with other employees.

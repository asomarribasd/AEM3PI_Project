PAID TIME OFF (PTO) POLICY

ANNUAL LEAVE ENTITLEMENT
Full-time employees receive 20 days of paid leave per year. Part-time employees receive 10 days. Unused leave can be carried over up to 5 days into the next year.

SICK LEAVE
Employees are entitled to 10 days of paid sick leave per year. Sick leave can be used for personal illness or family care. A doctor's note is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days.

PARENTAL LEAVE
New parents are entitled to 16 weeks of parental leave. This applies to both biological and adoptive parents. The first 8 weeks are paid at full salary, remaining 8 weeks at 50% salary.

LEAVE REQUEST PROCEDURE
Leave requests must be submitted at least 2 weeks in advance using the HR portal. Emergency leave should be communicated immediately to the manager. All leave requests require manager approval.

HOLIDAY SCHEDULE
The company observes the following public holidays: New Year's Day, Martin Luther King Jr. Day, Presidents' Day, Memorial Day, Independence Day, Labor Day, Thanksgiving (2 days), and Christmas.

BEREAVEMENT LEAVE
Employees are entitled to 5 days of paid bereavement leave in case of death of immediate family members (spouse, children, parents, siblings). Additional unpaid leave can be requested.

JURY DUTY
Employees called for jury duty will receive paid leave for the duration of their service. Employees must notify their manager immediately upon receiving a jury summons.

SABBATICAL LEAVE
After 10 years of service, employees may request a 3-month sabbatical leave at 30% pay. Sabbatical requests must be approved by HR and the executive team.

LEAVE WITHOUT PAY
Employees may request unpaid leave for personal reasons. Unpaid leave must be approved by both manager and HR. During unpaid leave, health insurance coverage may be affected.

RETURN TO WORK
Upon return from any leave exceeding 2 weeks, employees may be asked to participate in a return-to-work meeting with their manager to ensure smooth transition back to work.

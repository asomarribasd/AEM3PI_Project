COURSE REQUIREMENTS AND STUDENT EXPECTATIONS

COURSE TIME COMMITMENT
Budget 3-5 hours per week for self-paced courses. Live courses require 2-3 hours per week for classes plus 2-3 hours for assignments. Advanced courses may require more time. Plan your schedule accordingly.

ATTENDANCE EXPECTATIONS
For live courses, attendance is mandatory or recorded. Absences due to emergencies should be communicated to instructor. Recorded sessions available if you miss a live class. More than 20% absences may affect course completion.

ASSIGNMENT SUBMISSION
Assignments submitted through the course portal. Deadline is end of day on due date. Late submissions penalized 10% per day late. Submit original work only. Plagiarism detected through originality checker.

GRADING SCALE
A: 90-100%, B: 80-89%, C: 70-79%, D: 60-69%, F: Below 60%. Final grade calculation: Assignments 40%, Projects 40%, Final assessment 20%. Grading rubrics provided for all assessments.

PARTICIPATION AND ENGAGEMENT
Active participation in discussions and forums expected. Share insights and help fellow students. Instructor monitors participation and engagement. Low engagement may affect final grade for some courses.

PROJECT REQUIREMENTS
Capstone project required for most courses. Project guidelines provided in course materials. Projects demonstrate mastery of course concepts. Individual projects unless group project specified. Projects graded on creativity, functionality, and documentation.

CODE SUBMISSION STANDARDS
Submit code in specified format (GitHub, ZIP file, etc.). Code must be well-commented and documented. Follow language conventions and best practices. Code review provided as feedback. Incomplete or non-functional code results in lower grades.

ACADEMIC INTEGRITY
Original work required for all assignments. Collaboration encouraged but work must be your own. Cite sources if using external code or resources. Academic dishonesty results in failing grade for course.

DISCUSSION FORUM ETIQUETTE
Respectful communication required at all times. No harassment, discrimination, or offensive language. Stay on topic in discussion threads. Instructors monitor forums and moderate inappropriate posts.

COMMUNICATION WITH INSTRUCTORS
Questions answered within 24-48 hours during business days. Use course messaging system for communication. Email instructor for urgent matters. Office hours available for one-on-one questions.

TECHNICAL SUPPORT FOR COURSEWORK
Contact IT support for technical issues with platform. Troubleshooting available for software installation. Environment setup assistance provided. Alternative tools available if software doesn't work.

ACCOMMODATIONS FOR DISABILITIES
Accessibility features available in all courses. Request accommodations by submitting documentation. Extended time available for assessments. Alternative assessment formats may be available.

COURSE RESOURCES AND SUPPORT
Tutoring services available at no extra cost. Study groups can be formed with peers. Supplementary materials provided for struggling students. One-on-one coaching available for additional fee.

FEEDBACK AND COURSE EVALUATION
Feedback provided on all assignments before submission. Instructors available for questions about feedback. Course evaluation surveys at end of course. Student feedback used to improve courses.

INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS
Course materials are copyrighted and for personal use only. Redistribution of course materials prohibited. Student projects owned by student. Company projects may have different IP ownership.

CONTINUING EDUCATION CREDITS
Many courses offer continuing education credits. Credits awarded upon completion. Proof of completion provided for professional licensing. Check course details for credit availability.
